2.Why is this a Critical Part of the Interviewing Process?

One of the areas of computer science in which matrix multiplication is particularly useful is graphics, since a digital image is basically a matrix to begin with:
The rows and columns of the matrix correspond to rows and columns of pixels, and the numerical entries correspond to the pixels’ color values.
Decoding digital video, for instance, requires matrix multiplication; earlier this year, MIT researchers were able to build one of the first chips to implement the new high-efficiency video-coding standard for ultrahigh-definition TVs, in part because of patterns they discerned in the matrices it employs.

In the same way that matrix multiplication can help process digital video, it can help process digital sound.
A digital audio signal is basically a sequence of numbers, representing the variation over time of the air pressure of an acoustic audio signal.

Explained: Matrices -Larry Hardesty, MIT News Office

Other Major use cases of matrices include:
  • Machine learning
  • AI ( particularly in self driving cars)
  • 2D and 3D Graphics
In the case of graphics we need them to perform transformations such as scaling, rotation, and translation, on a potentially huge number of points and quickly within a 2D or 3D space.

lets say you have a 3D wheel made up of 100 triangles, each having 3 points, all relative to the origin (0,0,0) or "center" of the wheel.


in order to fixate or anchor this wheel to the body of your car
you will need to *translate* all of the points in your wheels 3-dimensional array relative to the car.
in fact, even chains of transformations (translate-> rotate-> scale-> translate) can be combined into a single matrix that does it all in one step.

3&5 How can I leverage this tool to increase my abilities as an Engineer and why do we need it?

Understanding matrices is essential to understanding graphics and many other complex fields of computer science. It opens the doors to understanding Camera coordination, rendering, model manipulation, deep learning, likely a great many of the future advancements yet to come in technology. Graphics boards have specialized hardware for doing matrix math, making this blindingly efficient - so you can’t really do 3D graphics without matrices. They sit as an anchor of knowledge for many directions you may want to take your career/business.
